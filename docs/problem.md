# Problem Statement — UniStack

## Context
INES-Ruhengeri students currently rely on WhatsApp groups as their primary channel for buying/selling items, finding housing near campus, and sharing announcements. This informal system creates systemic problems that affect student safety and quality of life.

## Problem
There is no dedicated, moderated digital platform for INES students to safely exchange goods, find housing, and stay informed. WhatsApp groups are unstructured, unmoderated, and ephemeral — making them unsuitable for marketplace transactions.

## Impact
- Students lose money to scammers who exploit anonymity
- Housing posts disappear before interested students see them
- Event announcements are buried and missed
- No accountability mechanism exists

## Proposed Solution
UniStack: a role-based web application accessible only to @ines.ac.rw users, where all posts are reviewed by moderators before going public, and a reporting system enables community self-governance.
